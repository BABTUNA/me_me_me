A new personal site is a strange thing to launch. The internet is already
absurdly full. There are a million blogs. Nobody is waiting for mine.

And yet — here we are.

## Why bother

I wanted a place that wasn't a feed. Somewhere I could put long thoughts
without them sliding under fourteen new things by morning. Somewhere I
could put work I'm proud of without algorithms deciding who sees it.

That place is just a website. Personal websites are good, actually.

## What lives here

Three kinds of things:

- **Work** — projects I've shipped, am shipping, or am tinkering with.
- **Writing** — essays, debugging stories, opinions I'll regret later.
- **About** — who I am and how to reach me.

That's it. No clever categories. No infinite scroll. No newsletter
popups. Just words and links.

## What to expect

I write irregularly. Sometimes a flood of three posts in a week, then
silence for two months. I'm not optimizing for cadence; I'm optimizing
for caring about what I put up.

Thanks for stopping by.
